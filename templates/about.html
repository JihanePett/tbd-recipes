My story

My name is Catherine, traveling is one of my passion and it has made me realize I am passionate about people.
I travel and I continue to travel not only to discover unknown places and amazing landscapes, to live experiences of a lifetime but also and especially to get to know a culture, to talk with the people and to discover their culture and their way of living.

Love for travels

This has always put me in a position to remember my smallness, to respect people, whoever they are and wherever they come from.
To a certain extent, it always pushes me to look myself in the face, to re-evaluate and readjust myself with who I really am because sometimes you get lost in the complexity of things and events in life, don't you? There is no trip or country I didn’t like; but most of the time, the relationship you get with people of a country makes the difference. By their history and culture, by their kindness, their character, their charisma... people and food is the main reason why I would go back again to a country.

Culinary exchanges

Of course, my choice goes first for countries where the human contact was rich and these countries are part of the list of countries where I have to go back because I remained with a taste of not enough. I still have so much to learn from these human contacts and exchanges. A lifetime will not be enough.
Probably, everything around cooking and kitchen provides me with similarities and differences with what I have already experienced and already known. It seems that this is what brings me back to my origins, getting to know myself and what allows me to appreciate what I am.
I still have so much to learn from these culinary exchanges.

James Baldwin

"I've met many people in Europe. I've even met myself there." James Baldwin “J’ai rencontré de nombreuses personnes en Europe. J’y ai même rencontré moi même.”
With hindsight, I have noticed that with another culture, another country, other people, gastronomy is my point of reference or comparison.
Through food, I can feel a sense of home and sense of community. Driven by a culinary curiosity, an interest in new tastes and original shapes my favourite go to place are the local markets and places, where i can discover special spices, local vegetables and fruits.
Whenever I have the opportunity, I sneak in local kitchens to furtively check on and learn cooking techniques. You understand, I love people, travels and food. Like many of us!
So I am happy to invite you to browse my pages, my recipes and I wait for your feedbacks, feel free to ask questions and exchange.

My Thermomix

My mother in law actually has the TM21 that was released in 1996. She always sang the praises of the kitchen appliance. She is the one and I thank her for that, who suggested me to have a Thermomix.
Six months after I got my little one, I felt just overwhelmed by our new life with a baby, not finding the time to cook as before.
While I was trying to get on a new routine as a new mum, my passion for cooking just faded away, I had no interest anymore in cooking or in sharing meals with friends,
I had no inspiration anymore and if we were not ordering ready meals, the basic food I would prepare back then was not appealing and flat.
My priorities had changed and I needed to adjust myself to our new life.
I needed help to save time for myself, get to know my little baby, eat properly, make real healthy meals for the family, and get ready for when I will be back to my full time job!
It was enough to rely on the ready meals; it was time more than ever to take care of our bodies and mind.
I found excuses to jump as it is an investment and it took a while to decide myself buying Thermomix. Initially it “was too much money for a simple blender!!!”
Then “it will take away the joy of food prep and the love of my pots and pans”, it will take me away from the kitchen, which is my “escape” from the daily worries!
Out of the three reasons, the last two points were not valid anymore as the pots and pans I was praising used to stay in the cupboards and trying to organize our healthy and weekly meal was stressful and time consuming.
